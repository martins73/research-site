About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Decisions, Operations, and Technology Management at the UCLA Anderson School of Management. My research focuses on Crowdwork platforms and the Supply Chain of Artificial Intelligence, with a specific interest in fairness and the ethical implications of these technologies.

Before returning to academia, I spent nearly a decade in the industry, working as a Management Consultant and later as the Head of Advanced Analytics at a major healthcare network in Chile. This experience grounded my research interests in the practical realities of how organizations implement and manage technology.

Beyond the research and the data, my life is defined by my family. I am incredibly fortunate to walk this path with my wife, Andrea, and our four children: Carlota (2017), Martín (2020), Joaquín (2024), and Juan Pablo (2025). They are my greatest joy, my daily chaos, and my most important responsibility.
