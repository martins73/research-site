About Me

I study strategy, search, and the dynamics of innovation and human capital.

Hello! I am a PhD Candidate in Management at UCLA Anderson School of Management, where I expect to graduate in 2027.

My research sits at the intersection of strategy and organizations. I use formal models and large-scale empirical analysis to study how firms and individuals search for valuable ideas, how performance is shaped by chance and capability, and how worker volatility affects human capital management.

Before returning to academia, I spent over a decade in industry at BlackLine, where I rose from Solutions Consultant to Chief Product Officer. That experience shapes how I think about strategy, product, and people inside organizations — and what questions I find worth asking as a researcher.

I hold an M.A. in Geopolitics and the Global Economy from University College Dublin and a B.A. in Geography from UCLA.
