# Research Notes System

## üéØ The Goal
Write 1 short research note per week (600-800 words) about AI ethics, crowdwork, labor economics, and related topics. Develop your thinking, educate your audience, spark conversation.

## üìÇ How to Use This Folder

### Files You'll Use
- **TEMPLATE.md** - Copy this every week to start a new post
- **ideas.md** - Your running tracker of topics, progress, and checklist
- **[DATE]-[SLUG].md** - Individual posts you write

### The Weekly 1-Hour System

**Tuesday (30 min): Pick Your Topic**
1. Open `ideas.md`
2. Scan recent papers, Twitter, arXiv in your fields:
   - Crowdwork/labor platform news
   - AI ethics debates
   - Related methods/research
3. Pick ONE topic that interests you
4. Write it in "This Week's Topic" section
5. Jot down 2-3 key points

**Wednesday-Thursday (45 min): Write**
1. Copy `TEMPLATE.md` to a new file: `YYYY-MM-DD-topic-slug.md`
   - Example: `2026-02-07-worker-retention-survey.md`
2. Fill in the template:
   - Hook (1-2 sentences)
   - Context (2-3 paragraphs)
   - Your insight (2-3 paragraphs)
   - Questions for readers
   - Further reading links
3. Aim for 600-800 words
4. Write conversationally, not academically

**Friday (15 min): Polish**
1. Proofread - tighten language, fix typos
2. Verify all links work
3. Update `ideas.md` - mark as "Done"
4. Stage & commit to git

### The Commit Pattern
```bash
git add _drafts/research-notes/
git commit -m "Add research note: [Topic Name]"
```

## ‚ú® Tips for Consistency

### Make It a Real Appointment
- Block "Tuesday 10am - Research notes time" on your calendar
- Recurring weekly reminder
- Treat it like a class you teach (non-negotiable)

### Remove Friction
- Don't overthink - aim for "clear & helpful," not perfect
- Use the template - no blank page paralysis
- Keep ideas.md open in your editor all week
- Write in Markdown in your favorite editor

### Track Your Progress
- Update ideas.md weekly - mark posts as done
- Watch your streak grow (motivated you to keep going)
- Share milestone: "10 posts written!" or "52 weeks in a row"

### Content Variety (Rotate Through These)
1. **Paper reactions** - Read a new paper, share your take
2. **Ethics & AI debates** - Comment on recent controversy
3. **Methodological insights** - "How to measure fairness"
4. **News commentary** - What does recent news mean?
5. **Reader questions** - Address questions from your field
