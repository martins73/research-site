The Input Log

The raw materials of research.

In Operations, we know that the variability of the output is determined by the quality of the input. My research is a function of the mental models, theological frameworks, and economic histories I consume. Here is the current queue.

The Human Supply Chain

JAN 2026
Ghost Work Mary L. Gray & Siddharth Suri

An essential ethnography of the on-demand economy. It humanizes the API. [SECRET LOG: Cyberpunk 2077 is the ultimate case study in supply chain failure. They released a beta product to meet a quarterly target. It is a warning: When you treat humans as components, the system glitches. Also, the story is a masterpiece.]

JAN 2026
Rerum Novarum Pope Leo XIII

The original manifesto on the rights of capital and labor (1891). It addresses the "misery and wretchedness pressing so unjustly on the majority of the working class." A necessary framework for analyzing AI crowdwork today.

JUL 2023
The Chile Project Sebastian Edwards

A rigorous history of the Chicago Boys and the downfall of neoliberalism. As a Chilean researcher, understanding this economic experiment is non-negotiable.

CONSTANT
Factory Physics Wallace Hopp & Mark Spearman

The definitive text on the mechanics of manufacturing. It replaces intuition with equations. Essential for understanding why "utilization" is a trap.

Formation

SEP 2025
Hannah's Children Catherine Pakaluk

"The women quietly defying the birth dearth." A data-driven look at large families in an era of decline. Resonates deeply with our own chaotic household of four.

CLASSIC
Man's Search for Meaning Viktor Frankl

The ultimate argument that humans are not just "resources" to be optimized, but souls searching for purpose. A check against pure efficiency.

Escapism

JAN 2026
A Confederacy of Dunces John Kennedy Toole

Ignatius J. Reilly is the ultimate anti-hero. A tragicomedy I finally tackled to start the year.

SERIES
The Thursday Murder Club Richard Osman

My go-to palate cleanser. I've read the first three. Perfect pacing, British humor, and a reminder that old age is not a decline.
