The Library

Garbage in, garbage out.

In Operations Research, we model systems based on their inputs. My academic output is a function of these inputs. This is a log of the mental models, theological frameworks, and escapist fiction currently in the queue.

The Foundation

CONSTANT
On Writing Well William Zinsser

The antidote to academic jargon. Zinsser taught me that clarity is the only virtue that matters. If the reader is lost, it is the writer's fault.

CONSTANT
Factory Physics Wallace Hopp & Mark Spearman

The definitive text on the mechanics of manufacturing. It replaces intuition with equations. Essential for understanding why "utilization" is a trap.

SECRET
Ghost Work Mary L. Gray & Siddharth Suri

An essential ethnography of the on-demand economy. It humanizes the API. [SECRET LOG: Cyberpunk 2077 is the ultimate case study in supply chain failure. They released a beta product to meet a quarterly target. It is a warning: When you treat humans as components, the system glitches. Also, the story is a masterpiece.]

Labor, Ethics & AI

JAN 2026
Rerum Novarum Pope Leo XIII

Written in 1891, but startlingly relevant to AI Crowdwork. It addresses the "misery and wretchedness pressing so unjustly on the majority of the working class." The original manifesto on the rights of capital and labor.

2024
Artificial Unintelligence Meredith Broussard

A critical look at "technochauvinism"—the belief that technology is always the solution. Essential reading for understanding the limits of the algorithms we build[cite: 26].

2023
The Chile Project Sebastian Edwards

A history of the Chicago Boys and the downfall of neoliberalism. Given my time at ACHS and my background, this is a necessary autopsy of an economic experiment.

2024
Going Infinite Michael Lewis

The rise and fall of SBF. A case study in what happens when "Effective Altruism" meets zero internal controls.

Theology & Formation

JAN 2026
Gay and Catholic Eve Tushnet

Accepting my sexuality and my faith.

2025
Hannah's Children Catherine Pakaluk

"The women quietly defying the birth dearth." A data-driven look at large families in an era of decline. Resonates with our own chaotic household of four.

2023
The Whole-Brain Child Daniel J. Siegel

Trying to apply systems thinking to the most unpredictable system of all: a toddler's brain[cite: 14].

Escapism

★★★★★
The Thursday Murder Club (Series) Richard Osman

My go-to palate cleanser. I've read The Bullet That Missed and The Man Who Died Twice. Perfect pacing, British humor, and a reminder that old age is not a decline.

CLASSIC
A Confederacy of Dunces John Kennedy Toole

Ignatius J. Reilly is the ultimate anti-hero. A tragicomedy I finally tackled in Jan 2026.

SPANISH
La sombra del viento Carlos Ruiz Zafón

"The Shadow of the Wind." A Gothic thriller about the Cemetery of Forgotten Books. A masterpiece of atmosphere[cite: 19].
